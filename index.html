What is new for the 2025 Tax Year?

The tax code changes in a lot of areas every year. For the 2025 tax year, the biggest things to look for are:

  • Child Credits have increased from $2,000 to $2,200 for eligible children (those under 16).
  • “Standard Deductions” increased for all taxpayers; and Seniors over 65 get an extra deduction for their seniority. (Helps people who do not itemize.)
  • Trump Account. Babies born in 2025 are eligible for a “Trump Account” (yes, that’s what it is called), which is $1,000 deposited into a tax deferred account. Get this: Report the newborn on Form 4547!
  • No tax on tips! For most people who receive tips as part of their wages (up to a limit of $25,000).
  • No tax on overtime! If your W2 shows overtime – or your pay stub – there is a deduction to make it tax free (within limits).
  • State and Local Tax (SALT) deduction limit increased to $40,000. This is huge! The previous limit was $10,000. Expect larger refunds for middle and high income people.
  • Form 1099-DA. For crypto currency transactions.
  • Form 1099K. For self-employed persons, and many others, Form 1099K reports income transactions from “payment apps” such as Venmo, PayPal, and CashApp. If you receive one or more 1099-K, be sure to send to us with your other tax documents.
  • Forms 1099-NEC. 1099-NEC forms report income from consulting or independent contract work and must be reported in your tax return. Be sure to itemize and add up the expenses you incurred in generating this income.
  • Forms 1099-R. These forms report retirement distributions. If you took an early withdrawal, new rules make these withdrawals exempt from the 10% federal penalty.
  • Forms 1099-C. These forms report “cancelled debts” which are treated as taxable income, unless you declare bankruptcy or insolvency (insolvency happens when your debts exceed your assets). Cancelled student loans are not taxable.
  • Electric Vehicle Credits. For tax year 2025, EV Credits have been transferred to the dealer. However, if the dealership gives you IRS Form 15400, show it to us because it may help your bottom line. EV credits were eliminated, entirely, for vehicles purchased after September 15, 2025.
  • Identity Theft Protections. One way to avoid Identity Theft in taxes is to obtain an IP PIN (Identity Protection Personal Identification Number) from the IRS website (www.irs.gov). (The only downside is that you’ll need a new IP PIN every year.)
